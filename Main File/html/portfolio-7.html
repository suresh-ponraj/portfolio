Learning Analytics

Learning analytics is the measurement, collection, analysis and reporting of data for learners and their contexts, for purposes of understanding and optimising learning and the environments in which it occurs so Course Insights is a internal tool of UQ which helps the academics to understand the Behaviour of the students in the course.

When I joined UQ Learning analytics team The Course Insights tool was in the early version in the hands of humans. So my role was to take that MVP to high end user tool with lot of workshop, user interviews with user and getting to know there problem and finding solution for that. So, we choose a solution of Design thinking and Co-design sessions for the user to understand what the pain are points they are facing while teaching a large class. We realised teaching large classes was the biggest deal they had to face as they won’t even remember the name of the students. And (Not surprisingly) the process of actually using analytics to inform pedagogical decisions is complex for large classes.

So, our key questions in Codesign session were

  1. In what ways do instructors make pedagogical decisions based on analytic data?
  2. What implications for LA design and implementation can be drawn based on this?

By Analysing all the data in Co-design sessions, we realised that The Course Insights Dashboard has to be remodeled to support Instructor’s pedagogical decision making. Also, along with design efforts, it is also important to consider implementation supports to facilitate translating information into actionable insights.

Our Collaboration with developers led to new models of dashboard (re)design and iterative improvement cycles.

THREE KEY DESIGN INGREDIENTS we used in Co-design session are:

  • WIDE PROBLEM SCOPE: The dashboard, data and academics are not the limit. Project objectives flexible from the beginning.
  • SAFE DESIGN SPACE: User led workshops for academics: a learning experience for everyone involved.
  • GENERATIVE TENSIONS: Leveraging tensions as fuel for design; going beyond "tell me what you want"

Take away from co-design session

Gathering (useful) input from humans to inform analytics is about much more than simply asking people what they would like Both efforts described here led to a variety of things we never would have imagined otherwise There was a concern with burdening already overstretched students and instructors, but they appreciated being involved in the processes.
